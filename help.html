How To Sync Your Tiles with TileIQ

For TileIQ to help you get those tiles, you must first generate a list of tiles, and sync it to your Garmin device.

  1. Generate Your Tile List

    The list of tiles you want to "hunt" must be generated and copied from the TileIQ WebApp:
    https://yauteiq.github.io/webapp/

    There are two ways you can generate your list:

    • Method 1: Hand Pick on the Map

      Simply use the map on the YauteIQ WebApp to select and manually add each tile you are interested in.
      You can make this easier by downloading your KML file from squadrats.com (on the right of the map screen) and uploading it on https://yauteiq.github.io/webapp/.
      This will overlay your visited tiles and squadratinhos on the map.

    • Method 2: Generate from a GPX route

      If your tile or squadrat hunting starts by planning a route in Strava, Garmin or RideWithGPS, this should be the best option for you.
      This option enables you to leverage the Squadrats Chrome extension to easily plan and mark tiles directly within your favourite route builder!
      Squadrats Route Planning Chrome Extension

      When planning your route, you can mark points of interest, which are then converted into your desired tile list.
      1. While planning your route, add a point in each of the tile/squadrat/squadratinho you wish to capture.
        • On Garmin Connect, they call it a "Course point"
        • On RideWithGPS, they call it a "Cue point"
        • On Strava, they call it a "Waypoint"
        2. Unfortunately, Komoot doesn't offer a way to customise waypoints, so this method is not available for Komoot routes.
      2. Name each point with a name starting with one or two underscores (`_` or `__`) to signal your intent:
        • One underscore (e.g., `_mytile`): Signals interest in the tile/squadrats the point belongs to.
        • Two underscores (e.g., `__mysquadratinho`): Signals interest in the squadratinhos the point belongs to.
      3. Export the route as a GPX file.
      4. Upload the GPX file to the YauteIQ WebApp. A list will be generated containing all the tiles corresponding to your custom points.

  2. Copy and Sync the List

    Once the TileIQ WebApp displays the list of tile IDs you want to hunt, click the button to copy it.

    The rest happens in Garmin Connect:

    1. In the Garmin Connect App, find and open your Garmin device.
    2. Navigate to Activities, Apps & More > Data fields.
    3. Select and open the settings for the TileIQ data field.
    4. Paste the text list you copied into the settings field.
    5. Sync your device
    6. Go get those tiles!
